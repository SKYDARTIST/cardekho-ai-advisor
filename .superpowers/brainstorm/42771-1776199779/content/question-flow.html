How should we ask the buyer questions?

Three approaches — each creates a different feeling for the user

💬

A — Classic Chat

Type answers like ChatGPT. Open-ended. "Tell me about your life..."

🤖 Tell me a little about yourself — how do you use a car day to day?
I drive kids to school, then go to office. Highway on weekends.
🤖 Got it — family car, city + highway mix. What's your budget?
✓ Feels personal ✗ Hard to extract specs
🃏

B — Card Wizard (Recommended)

One emotional question at a time. Big visual cards to tap. 4 questions total.

Question 2 of 4 — "Who rides with you?"
🧑
Just me
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦
Family ✓
👫
Me + partner
🐕
Me + pets
✓ Feels emotional ✓ Easy to build ✓ Clean AI input
🎭

C — Personality Quiz

"Which driver are you?" — vibe-based, like BuzzFeed meets car buying

What does your ideal Sunday drive look like?
🏔️ Mountain roads, windows down, no destination
🏙️ City cruise, good music, AC on full blast
🏡 Quick errands, kids in back, arrive in one piece
✓ Most engaging ~ Harder to map to specs
★ Insight — The 4 Questions (for B or C)
Q1 — Daily Drive
City chaos / Highway runs / Both equally
Q2 — Who Rides
Just me / Partner / Family / Pets
Q3 — What Matters
Comfort / Fuel savings / Boot space / Style
Q4 — Budget
Under ₹8L / ₹8–15L / ₹15–25L / ₹25L+
